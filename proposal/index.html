Summary

Our project’s main focus is on modeling the dynamic simulation of lava using the Navier-Stokes equations. We plan to have our fluid (lava) flow down the sides of our volcano.

Problem Description

In general, the problem we are ought to solve is the following - having to model a viscous fluid (lava) as it traverses down our mountainous terrain. We claim that it’s important as it can have direct implications for real-life scenarios, and it can be particularly useful for areas that are prone to this behavior such as Hawaii.

Although our simulation won’t account for all the complexity that can potentially lie on a volcano (trees, bushes, houses, rock formations, etc), it should provide a general intuition for the expected flow of lava down the mountain. This project is challenging in that we have to use the navier-stokes equations as well as model how the lava will interact with our terrain as it flows down. Complexity can scale exponentially depending on realistic we decide to make this terrain.
